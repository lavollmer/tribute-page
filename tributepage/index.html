Grace Murray Hopper

Grace Murray Hopper (1906-1992) was a pioneering computer scientist and naval officer who made significant contributions to the field of computer programming. Here are some key details about her life and achievements:

Early Life and Education:

  • Grace Hopper was born on December 9, 1906, in New York City, USA.
  • She earned her bachelor's degree in mathematics and physics from Vassar College in 1928.
  • Hopper then went on to complete her master's degree and Ph.D. in mathematics at Yale University in 1930 and 1934, respectively.

Contributions to Computer Science:

  • Hopper played a crucial role in the development of the COBOL (Common Business-Oriented Language) programming language, which became widely used for business applications.
  • She advocated for the idea of writing programs in a language closer to English rather than machine code, making programming more accessible to non-technical users.
  • Hopper's contributions to computer programming and software development methodologies had a lasting impact on the field.
